Inventory guide

Estate Inventory and Photo Checklist

The archived photo gallery listed collectibles, costume jewelry, paintings, porcelain, furnishings, toys, silver, rugs, primitives, clocks, furniture, and decorative objects. This rebuild turns it into a working documentation checklist.

Photo sequence

  1. Room overview: photograph each wall, closet, cabinet, shelf, and drawer before moving anything.
  2. Category photos: group similar items together: jewelry, silver, art, books, toys, rugs, tools, glass, pottery, and furniture.
  3. Detail photos: capture signatures, maker marks, labels, hallmarks, edition numbers, condition issues, and accessories.
  4. Scale photos: include a ruler, measuring tape, or neutral object when size affects value.
  5. Record photos: photograph receipts, appraisals, certificates, old labels, and family notes next to the item.

Category checklist

Jewelry and silver

Marks, clasp, stone condition, weight if available, boxes, receipts, appraisals, flatware patterns, and hollowware damage.

Paintings and prints

Front, back, signature, medium, labels, frame, edition, condition, dimensions, and provenance notes.

Porcelain, pottery, glass

Maker marks, form, chips, cracks, repairs, crazing, glaze, shape number, pattern name, and matched sets.

Furniture and rugs

Dimensions, labels, joinery, hardware, underside, repairs, odors, stains, pile wear, edges, and back construction.

Toys and games

Boxes, instructions, missing parts, battery compartments, paint, moving parts, maker marks, and accessory matches.

Books and paper

Title pages, copyright pages, dust jackets, inscriptions, plates, maps, condition, and whether papers are private family records.

File naming

Use simple filenames such as room-category-number: `living-art-001`, `garage-tools-012`, `jewelry-ring-003`. Keep a matching spreadsheet with description, condition, location found, and follow-up needed.

Privacy filter

Do not publish or share photos that show personal documents, account numbers, prescriptions, license plates, family letters, passports, or private photographs. Those should be separated before any buyer-facing gallery is made.
