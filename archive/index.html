Archive methodology

Clean Midnight Sun Archive

The Web Archive shows that this domain had an early antique appraisal and estate-sale identity, then a later unrelated WordPress layer. The useful antique and auction URLs were rebuilt; spam and commercial guest posts were not.

What was restored

Restored pages cover antique appraisal services, estate liquidation, live auction preparation, photo documentation, vintage toys, furniture, primitives, jewelry, artwork, silver, books, household goods, and collectible category checklists.

The old pages were not copied directly. They contained obsolete Yahoo layout code, old tracking scripts, private addresses, phone numbers, emails, mailto links, and direct links to commercial auction pages. The rebuilt version keeps the topic value and removes those risks.

What was excluded

The later WordPress posts about waterproofing, roofing, cleaning, removalists, retail fit-outs, shower screens, garage doors, scales, and similar services were excluded. They are off-topic for the domain history and had commercial outbound anchors. Those URLs now resolve to the noindex 404 page instead of being revived as SEO spam.

How to use this archive

Start with the appraisal guide if you need documented value. Start with the estate sale guide if you are clearing a household. Start with the photo checklist if you need to triage rooms quickly. Use the individual collection pages when a sale includes toys, furniture, primitives, art, jewelry, books, silver, or mixed household objects.

Restoration Rules

  • Keep pages with repeated historical antique, appraisal, estate-sale, or auction relevance.
  • Rewrite pages into useful guidance instead of copying obsolete layouts.
  • Remove personal contact details, exact private addresses, and outdated calls to contact the old company.
  • Keep useful source links behind JavaScript buttons, not direct HTML anchors.
  • Exclude unrelated commercial WordPress content from sitemap and internal links.
